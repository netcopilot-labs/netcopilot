NetCopilot is open-source Network Context Intelligence — a deterministic, verifiable model of your multi-vendor network that humans, LLMs, and agents can all query for grounded answers.

It collects from your network read-only, builds one canonical graph model of what's actually there — devices, links, VLANs, routing, firewall policy — and exposes it so you can ask questions in plain English and get one answer, with an evidence trace showing exactly where each fact came from.

The core idea — context, never actuation. NetCopilot never changes your network. It produces deterministic truth and context; you, or your agent, decide and act on it. Deterministic systems produce the truth; the AI explains it — never the other way around.

What makes it different:

Every answer is grounded in evidence collected from your actual devices — never invented, never guessed. Each fact traces back to the exact output that produced it.
When an answer comes from vendor documentation, the source PDF and page are cited.
When it doesn't have an answer, it says so. It does not fabricate.
It runs on your infrastructure, with your data, under your control. Nothing leaves your network unless you choose it to.
It is multi-vendor — Cisco IOS-XE / IOS-XR and Fortinet today — and built to be extended.
It speaks Model Context Protocol, so any LLM, agent, or automation platform can consume the same model directly.

NetCopilot is not a monitoring tool, and it does not act on the network. It is the deterministic source of truth the rest of your tooling — and your AI — can build on. Open-source, Apache-2.0.
